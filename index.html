02 · The Project

A cart that follows you

It began with a familiar problem: a mountain of beach gear and soft sand that swallowed every wheel thrown at it. The answer became a full engineering project — a hand-welded aluminum cart on balloon tires, sized to haul 400+ lb of gear without complaint.

Nearly every part is designed, printed, wired, or written in-house: a drivetrain tuned to ease in smoothly under load, distributed power systems that keep sensitive electronics stable beside high-current motors, and enclosures built to survive sand and sun.

Then it got ambitious. A wearable UWB tag and four corner receivers fix the operator's position so the cart drives itself — hands-free — while a spinning 360° scanner steers it around people and stops it at curbs. Prefer to drive? A switch on the remote hands over full manual control.

05 · Additive Manufacturing

Wheel hubs in carbon fiber

The stock hubs developed play under full motor torque — and when the wheels came apart, the PETG spacers had cracked clean through. The fix discards the factory hub entirely: each hub prints as two identical halves in carbon-fiber-reinforced nylon that lock into one solid body — mounting bell, tire barrel, and bearing boss in a single piece.
